# Platformer

A 2D platformer built in Unity — started as a hackathon project at Deltatime GameJam (Parallax, PESU EC), rebuilt and improved after a year of experience with the engine.

> Built by Aryan Burman · PES University Bengaluru

---

## About

Participated in Deltatime GameJam twice. The first time I was just getting introduced to Unity. By the second jam, the same concept came out significantly better — better level design, tighter mechanics, and a clearer vision of what I wanted to build.

After the jam I continued improving it, but eventually hit a wall with AI-generated assets that didn't suit the visual style I wanted. That friction pushed me toward 3D — leading directly to Forest Survival, where I dropped generated assets entirely and focused on systems architecture instead.

---

## What I learned

- How much difference a year of experience makes on the same problem
- Player animations require careful sprite scaling — spritesheet dimensions directly affect how the character sits relative to the ground
- Box collider placement needs to align precisely with the sprite's feet, not the sprite's center — a small detail that took real experimentation to get right
- Why asset quality matters more than asset quantity
- When to stop polishing and start rebuilding — which led to Forest Survival
